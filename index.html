Task 02 · Brand Review

The thinking runs deep. The world isn't built yet.

"Deeper side of gaming" is yours to own. The chart is sound. The voyage simply hasn't been made.

What works

The strategy document is real thinking

The pyramid. The archetypes. The Brand IS / IS NOT table. This is thought, not a template. And almost none of it has surfaced yet.

"Depth" is unclaimed territory

Everyone claims innovation. Everyone claims authenticity. Nobody claims depth — as a worldview, not a feature. The lagoon holds both: deep product, deep team.

The colour & logo system can be recognised

Teal. Navy. A tentacle. No one else in iGaming looks like this. It only needs to surface every time — not appear in one post and vanish from the next.

What to improve

The SWOT under-rates the threats

Regulation. A crowded sea. Hyperproduction. Thin operator visibility. All filed as afterthoughts. Knowing where the hull is weak is how a vessel stays afloat.

"Authentic" is said so often it stops meaning anything

Write "we are authentic" and you have already lost it. Authenticity is a behaviour, never a word. Cut to the two or three things only Black Lagoon can say. Build from there.

AI is a tool, not yet a capability

Today AI makes the pictures. That is all. For a studio hiring an AI Creative Specialist, that is the thing to change: AI should be how we think, not only how we render.

Task 02 · Brand Evolution

From documents to world. Six months.

Close the gap not by adding language, but by removing what's generic — and letting the conviction show.

Month 1–2

Strip & clarify

Begin with one true sentence: "We came here to take slot games deeper." Hold every published word against it. If a line could belong to any studio, rewrite it until it could only be Black Lagoon. No new creative until the foundation means something.

Month 3–4

Monster definition

Not a logo refresh — an expedition. Who is the monster? What does it want? How does it speak? The output is a character: a voice, a role, a face that grows out of the silhouette. By month four it speaks in a post — and the room knows who it is.

Month 5–6

AI as brand signal

Build the Google AI Studio pipeline. Document it. Make it repeatable — then make it visible. A studio that can show how its AI thinks is proving the word "deeper," not merely saying it.

Task 02 · Monster Concept

Captain Nemo · Prince Dakkar

Not a mascot. Not a villain.
An outlaw intellectual.

He left the surface not because it beat him, but because he understood it too well. So he built something better, in the dark, by his own rules. He commands absolute loyalty. He has mastered what no one above has thought to study. He owes the surface no explanation — and offers none.

That's the Black Lagoon Monster.

Character

Not threatening. Commanding. He doesn't hunt — he watches. He has been down here long enough to read probability the way no surface mathematician can. When he moves, he is precise. When he speaks, it is rare, and final. And he finds the surface's hurry quietly amusing.

Visual direction

Painterly, never photoreal. Comic-book bones — the Dick Tracy line. Deep navy, bioluminescent teal, a thread of gold. Partly seen, never fully shown. One eye. A hint of scale. A coin in hand, a reel in view, or simply watching from below.

Role in communications

Milestone posts · game-launch narrative ("a new game enters the lagoon") · partnership moments · eventually a consistent narrator voice operators recognize and look for.

Voice — first person · minimal · declarative

I watched 1,000,000× happen. I've seen bigger.

A new game enters the lagoon.

You set the bet. I set the math.
