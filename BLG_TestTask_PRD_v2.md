## CONTEXT SNAPSHOT

**Black Lagoon Games** is an independent iGaming studio, Alpine HQ, Stockholm-registered, 11–50 people. They release two new games per week with six titles in the pipeline at any given time. They build game *families*, not single titles — entire ecosystems with shared math, mechanics, and visual DNA. Their markets are Africa, LATAM, CEE, CIS.

What makes them different isn't the games themselves — slot games look like slot games. It's the R&D depth (20% PhDs), the mathematical innovation underneath, and the conviction that drives it. They don't follow the industry. They go deeper than it.

Culturally: startup speed meets farmer logic. You ship because it needs shipping, not because a process said so. Infinite freedom paired with infinite responsibility. Low turnover. High loyalty. A small team that takes care of each other beyond the job description.

In the interview, someone mentioned Dick Tracy. That's not a throwaway reference — it's a signal about how they see their own visual world: comic book energy, bold characters, a noir that's playful not pretentious.

**The one line that already exists and says everything:**
*"We didn't come here to follow rules. We came to take slot games deeper."*

Everything else — the lagoon, the monster, the math — flows from that.

**Brand Essence:** Deeper side of gaming
**Tagline:** Beyond the reels. Beneath the surface.
**Tone:** Commanding intelligence. Wise, not just curious. Confident without explaining itself.
**Visual language:** BLG teal/navy palette, comic-book noir, cinematic atmosphere, tentacle/monster mythos, the depth and mystery of what lives below the surface — not as decoration, but as worldview.
**Monster:** Currently expressed through silhouette and atmosphere. Not yet a defined character. Defining it properly is a 6-month world-building project — not something done in a brief.

**LinkedIn snapshot (June 2026):**
- 1,231 followers / 8 associated members visible
- Active posting cadence, mix of game launches, milestones, partnerships, atmospheric video content
- Best content: the human moments — team hiking Triglav, scuba diving, making fools of themselves. That's where the real brand lives.
- Problem: as they grow and chase operators, the corporate speak creeps in. That's the thing to protect against.

## TASK 1: LinkedIn Audit & Mini Campaign

### 1.1 What Works

**The atmospheric video content is genuinely category-breaking.**
Dark cinematic AI-generated visuals — the lagoon surface at night, the monster silhouette, the coin descending into the deep — don't look like anything else in iGaming. Most studios post flat game screenshots. BLG posts *mood*. This is the right instinct, executed well.

**The human moments are where the brand actually lives.**
The SiGMA Rome post (team, merch, Colosseum). Triglav. Scuba diving. "Veni. Vidi. Vici." These perform because they're real. The brand strategy talks about authenticity at length — but these posts *are* authentic without trying to be. More of this, structured.

**Partnership copy shows the brand voice at its best.**
"BlueOcean Gaming x Black Lagoon Games — Diving into deeper waters together." One line. Uses the metaphor without forcing it. Doesn't explain itself. This is what B2B LinkedIn should sound like from them.

---

### 1.2 What to Improve

**The About section reads like a feature list, not a brand.**
"New Age Casino Games Studio. At Black Lagoon, we don't just create games..." — this could be any studio. "Deeper side of gaming" appears nowhere in the public-facing copy. The commanding intelligence in the internal brand documents hasn't made it to the page operators actually read. This is the most important single fix.

**The content has no system — it's all launches and milestones.**
There's no recurring content pillar that builds the world between game releases. No thought leadership, no team voice, no story thread that operators follow week to week. The result is a page that grows on events, not on ideas. That means growth stops when the launch calendar pauses.

**The team isn't amplifying each other.**
8 associated members, all relatively passive on LinkedIn. A simple internal system — a Slack channel where someone posts when BLG publishes, and the team likes/reposts within the first hour — could 3-4x organic reach on every post with zero budget. First-hour engagement is what LinkedIn's algorithm rewards. This is the lowest-effort, highest-impact fix on the list.

*Note: The featured post (SiGMA Rome, 7 months old) should be replaced with something that directly explains to an operator why BLG exists — in brand voice, not sales copy.*

### 1.3 Mini Campaign: *WHAT LIVES BENEATH*

**Goal:** Build world awareness and operator brand recognition over 30 days — not follower count metrics, but quality of attention. The right operator seeing this once is worth more than 300 generic follows.

**Concept:**
A 4-week serialized campaign that surfaces the Black Lagoon Monster as an emerging presence — not a mascot reveal, a mythology in progress. Each week, one "chapter" appears. The monster isn't explained. It's felt. Operators and industry followers start to recognize it. By week 4, they're looking for the next post.

This runs in parallel with regular game launches — additive, not a replacement.

**Weekly arc:**

| Week | Chapter | Format | Signal |
|------|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | *It Stirs* | 10s atmospheric video | "Something beneath the surface has been watching." |
| 2 | *It Calculates* | Static visual + copy | "Every reel it touches becomes legendary. It ran the math." |
| 3 | *It Speaks* | Short video, first-person voice | "You thought the odds were against you. I set the odds." |
| 4 | *It Surfaces* | Concept art reveal | "This is what lives in the Black Lagoon. Properly." |

**Sample post — Week 1:**

Hook: *"The waters have been quiet. Not for long."*

Body: *"Somewhere in the Black Lagoon, something that understands probability better than any mathematician is awake. It doesn't pitch. It doesn't follow up. It just knows what happens when the math goes past the reels. Week 1 of 4."*

Visual: AI-generated (Gemini/Veo) — lagoon surface at night, single ripple, bioluminescent depth visible below. No logo. No text overlay.

AI production: Gemini for image/video generation, Claude for copy iteration, Google AI Studio for prompt system documentation.

## TASK 2: Brand Evolution & Monster Concept

### 2.1 What Works

**The brand strategy document is sophisticated.** The pyramid, the archetype model, the Brand IS / IS NOT table — this is real brand thinking, not a template. The foundation is solid. The problem is that almost none of it has made it from the document into the world yet.

**"Deeper side of gaming" is genuinely ownable.** In a category where everyone claims innovation and authenticity, nobody claims *depth* — as a worldview, not a feature. The lagoon metaphor is generative. It works for operators (depth of product) and for culture (depth of team). It's the right territory.

**The color system and logomark have strong recognition potential.** The teal/navy/tentacle system is distinctive. There's no direct visual competitor in iGaming doing this. The system just needs consistent application — right now it exists in some posts and disappears in others.

---

### 2.2 What to Improve

**The SWOT analysis doesn't take threats seriously enough.** The threats section is thin — regulatory changes, competitive market, operational complexity listed almost as afterthoughts. A studio this ambitious in a market this crowded needs sharper threat awareness, especially around game hyperproduction (where everyone is releasing constantly) and their limited operator visibility. Knowing where you're weak is how you stay sharp.

**The word "authentic" appears so many times it starts to undermine itself.** If you have to write "we are authentic" in a brand document, you haven't yet found the behavior that makes authenticity obvious. The brand pyramid is well-structured, but large sections of the copy are generic — it could describe many iGaming studios with the name changed. The fix isn't more language. It's stripping back to the two or three things that are *only* true of BLG and building from there.

**AI is listed as an opportunity but isn't visible as a capability.** The brand strategy mentions "AI-driven processes" as an opportunity in the SWOT. But right now AI is just a production tool — it makes the visuals. It isn't part of the brand story. For a studio hiring an AI Creative Specialist, this is the thing to change: AI should be *how we think*, not just *how we make things*.

---

### 2.3 Brand Evolution — 6 Months

**Direction: From documents to world.**

The internal brand thinking is good. The external expression of it isn't there yet. The job over six months is to close that gap — not by adding more brand language, but by removing what's generic and letting the real conviction show.

**Month 1–2: Strip and clarify.**
Start with the single true sentence: *"We came here to take slot games deeper."* Audit every public touchpoint — LinkedIn About, website, partnership announcements, game launch copy — against that sentence. If a piece of copy couldn't only come from BLG, rewrite it until it could. No new creative until the foundation says what it means.

**Month 3–4: Monster definition.**
Not a logo refresh — a world-building process. Who is the monster? What does it want? How does it speak? This requires a workshop, not a brief. The output is a character document that gives the monster a voice, a role in communications, and a visual direction that evolves from the silhouette into something with presence. Target: by month 4, the monster has spoken in a post and the audience recognizes it.

**Month 5–6: AI as brand signal.**
Build and document the Google AI Studio production pipeline. Make it replicable. Then make the pipeline itself visible — not as a case study, but as proof of how BLG thinks. A studio that can show its AI creative process is demonstrating the "deeper" claim, not just stating it.

### 2.4 Monster Concept: Captain Nemo

Not a mascot. Not a villain. An outlaw intellectual.

Captain Nemo — Prince Dakkar — abandoned the surface world not because he failed at it, but because he understood it too well. He built something better in the depths. He operates by his own rules, commands absolute loyalty, and has mastered things others haven't even thought to study. He doesn't explain himself to the surface. He doesn't need to.

That's the Black Lagoon Monster.

**Character direction:**
Not threatening — *commanding*. The monster doesn't hunt. It observes. It has been in the lagoon long enough to understand probability at a level no PhD has reached on the surface. When it acts, it's precise. When it speaks, it's rare and final. It has the dry wit of someone who finds the surface world's urgency faintly amusing.

**Visual direction:**
Painterly, not photorealistic. Comic-book DNA (the Dick Tracy reference belongs here). Deep navy, bioluminescent teal, gold — the existing BLG palette but given depth and character. The monster is partially visible, never fully revealed. One eye. The suggestion of scale. It holds a coin, or watches a reel, or simply observes from below.

**Voice:**
First person. Minimal. Declarative.
- "I watched 1,000,000x happen. I've seen bigger."
- "A new game enters the lagoon."
- "You set the bet. I set the math."

**Role in communications:**
- Milestone posts (when something record-breaking happens)
- Game launch narrative ("a new game enters the lagoon")
- Partnership moments (the monster surfaces when something interesting happens above)
- Eventually: a consistent narrator voice that operators recognize and look for

**AI concept brief (Imagen/Gemini):**
*"Ancient deep-sea entity, partially submerged, one large eye visible above water line, bioluminescent teal markings, holding a glowing casino coin, painterly comic-book style, deep navy and teal palette, gold accent light, cinematic composition, partially revealed never fully shown, noir underwater atmosphere, no text"*
