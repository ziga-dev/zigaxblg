## Section 1 — LinkedIn Audit

- **Eyebrow:** Task 01 · LinkedIn Audit
- **Title:** The brand is already breathing. It just lives in the human moments.
- **Lede:** The instinct is right. The system isn't built yet. Three soundings up, three down.

### Column: What works

1. **The atmospheric video breaks the category**
   The lagoon at night. A silhouette. A coin sinking into black water. Made with AI, unlike anything else in iGaming. Most studios show the machine. This shows the deep. The instinct is right — and so is the execution.

2. **The human moments are the real brand**
   Rome. Triglav. Scuba tanks. "Veni. Vidi. Vici." These land because they are true. Nobody is performing authenticity here — they are simply being it. Do more of it. On purpose.

3. **The partnership copy, at its best**
   _"Diving into deeper waters together."_
   One line. The metaphor does the rowing. Nothing is explained. This is the voice — find it again, and keep it.

### Column: What to improve

1. **The About section reads like a feature list**
   "Deeper side of gaming" lives in the strategy deck. It appears nowhere a customer can read it. The conviction never made the climb to the surface. Fix this one first.

2. **The content has no system**
   Launches and milestones. Nothing in the water between them. The page rises with the calendar and goes still when it pauses. A brand needs a pulse between events.

3. **The crew isn't amplifying each other**
   Eight aboard, all quiet. One Slack channel. Likes and reposts in the first hour, while the algorithm is watching. Three to four times the reach, for nothing. The easiest win on the page.

- **Note (callout):** Swap the featured post — SiGMA Rome, seven months cold — for one that tells an operator _why Black Lagoon exists_. In its own voice. Not a sales sheet.

## Section 3 — Brand Review

- **Eyebrow:** Task 02 · Brand Review
- **Title:** The thinking runs deep. The world isn't built yet.
- **Lede:** "Deeper side of gaming" is yours to own. The chart is sound. The voyage simply hasn't been made.

### Column: What works

1. **The strategy document is real thinking**
   The pyramid. The archetypes. The Brand IS / IS NOT table. This is thought, not a template. And almost none of it has surfaced yet.

2. **"Depth" is unclaimed territory**
   Everyone claims innovation. Everyone claims authenticity. Nobody claims _depth_ — as a worldview, not a feature. The lagoon holds both: deep product, deep team.

3. **The colour & logo system can be recognised**
   Teal. Navy. A tentacle. No one else in iGaming looks like this. It only needs to surface every time — not appear in one post and vanish from the next.

### Column: What to improve

1. **The SWOT under-rates the threats**
   Regulation. A crowded sea. Hyperproduction. Thin operator visibility. All filed as afterthoughts. Knowing where the hull is weak is how a vessel stays afloat.

2. **"Authentic" is said so often it stops meaning anything**
   Write "we are authentic" and you have already lost it. Authenticity is a behaviour, never a word. Cut to the two or three things only Black Lagoon can say. Build from there.

3. **AI is a tool, not yet a capability**
   Today AI makes the pictures. That is all. For a studio hiring an AI Creative Specialist, that is the thing to change: AI should be _how we think_, not only how we render.

## Section 4 — Brand Evolution

- **Eyebrow:** Task 02 · Brand Evolution
- **Title:** From documents to world. Six months.
- **Lede:** Close the gap not by adding language, but by removing what's generic — and letting the conviction show.

### Phases

1. **Month 1–2 — Strip & clarify**
   Begin with one true sentence: "We came here to take slot games deeper." Hold every published word against it. If a line could belong to any studio, rewrite it until it could only be Black Lagoon. No new creative until the foundation means something.

2. **Month 3–4 — Monster definition**
   Not a logo refresh — an expedition. Who is the monster? What does it want? How does it speak? The output is a character: a voice, a role, a face that grows out of the silhouette. By month four it speaks in a post — and the room knows who it is.

3. **Month 5–6 — AI as brand signal**
   Build the Google AI Studio pipeline. Document it. Make it repeatable — then make it visible. A studio that can show how its AI thinks is _proving_ the word "deeper," not merely saying it.

## Section 5 — Monster Concept

- **Eyebrow:** Task 02 · Monster Concept
- **Tag:** Captain Nemo · Prince Dakkar `[keep names]`
- **Title:** Not a mascot. Not a villain. An outlaw intellectual.
- **Intro:** He left the surface not because it beat him, but because he understood it too well. So he built something better, in the dark, by his own rules. He commands absolute loyalty. He has mastered what no one above has thought to study. He owes the surface no explanation — and offers none.
- **Intro (accent):** That's the Black Lagoon Monster.

### Details

- **Character:** Not threatening. Commanding. He doesn't hunt — he watches. He has been down here long enough to read probability the way no surface mathematician can. When he moves, he is precise. When he speaks, it is rare, and final. And he finds the surface's hurry quietly amusing.
- **Visual direction:** Painterly, never photoreal. Comic-book bones — the Dick Tracy line. Deep navy, bioluminescent teal, a thread of gold. Partly seen, never fully shown. One eye. A hint of scale. A coin in hand, a reel in view, or simply watching from below.
- **Role in communications:** Milestone posts · game-launch narrative ("a new game enters the lagoon") · partnership moments · eventually a consistent narrator voice operators recognize and look for.

### Voice (first person · minimal · declarative) `[canonical brand lines — transcreate carefully]`

- "I watched 1,000,000× happen. I've seen bigger."
- "A new game enters the lagoon."
- "You set the bet. I set the math."
